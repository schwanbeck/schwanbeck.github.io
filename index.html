Introduction: Initial Aim of the Project

  • Almost all sequenced C. difficile strains possess one chemoreceptor and the chemotaxis machinery needed for signal sensing and transduction (MCP, CheY, CheW)
  • Flagellar motility is a requirement for chemotaxis
  • A detailed analysis of the chemotactic behavior requires observation of the motility pattern of single cells for example “run and tumble” in E. coli
